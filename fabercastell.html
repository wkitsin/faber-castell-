The noble heritage of Graf von Faber-Castell

Founded in 1761 by Kaspar Faber and ambitiously expanded by his great-grandson Baron Lothar von Faber, Faber-Castell is a brand synonymous today with high-quality writing instruments. With the marriage of Baron von Faber's granddaughter Ottilie von Faber to cadet of the Counts of Castell Count Alexander zu Castell-Rüdenhausen in 1898, Faber-Castell has grown from strength to strength over 250 years, with its products still standing out as being the choice of professionals and artists worldwide.
Of the many product lines under the umbrella of Faber-Castell, Graf von Faber-Castell is the very finest. Covering pencils and pens (fountain, ballpoint and rollerball) made from exotic materials such as rare woods, resign and ivory, Graf von Faber-Castell was created by the family's eighth generation member (and former CEO) Anton-Wolfgang Graf von Faber-Castell. "I was inspired by the writing instruments produced by my ancestors," he said. "And I saw it as a personal challenge to rediscover these products of a past age, and to bring them up to date with timesless design and the use of modern technology." In Graf von Faber-Castell, the company has successfully achieved that. With its exemplary selection of beautifully-crafted and meticulously-engineered writing instruments, the spirit and ambitions of Kasper Faber and Lothar von Faber live on in these beautiful pens.
